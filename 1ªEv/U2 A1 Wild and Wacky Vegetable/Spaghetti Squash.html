Spaghetti Squash: Nature's Pasta

Unlike its name, spaghetti squash isn't pasta — but when cooked, its golden strands look and feel surprisingly similar! Low in calories, rich in nutrients, perfect for a healthy alternative.

Healthy Living Magazine

Fun Facts

Property Detail
Origin North & Central America
Main Nutrient Vitamin A & Fiber
Calories (per cup) 42

Whole Squash

Golden and oval, ready to transform into veggie noodles.

Cooked Perfection

Once baked, the flesh separates into long, tender strands.

Ready to Serve

Perfect with tomato sauce, parmesan, or garlic butter.

Simple Recipe

Ingredients: 1 spaghetti squash, olive oil, salt, pepper, and your favorite sauce.

Instructions

  • Preheat oven to 200°C (400°F).
  • Cut squash in half lengthwise and scoop out seeds.
  • Drizzle with olive oil, season with salt and pepper.
  • Place face down on baking sheet and bake for 40 minutes.
  • Use a fork to scrape inside — spaghetti-like strands appear!
  • Top with sauce, cheese, or pesto and enjoy.
